Problem Statement

Shipping AI agents is still too manual, fragmented, and hard to trust.

Teams can get a useful model output quickly. What they still do not get is a reusable product workflow with tooling, persistence, discoverability, and visibility into what actually happened.

01

Fragmented setup

Teams juggle model SDKs, auth, tools, and glue code before they can ship anything usable.

02

One-off prompts

Useful AI behavior often stays trapped in chat history instead of becoming reusable product logic.

03

No observability

Without run history, tool traces, and cost visibility, teams cannot reliably debug or trust agent output.

04

Slow iteration

Every new agent idea means rebuilding packaging, storage, and operating surfaces from scratch.

Why teams stall Good AI demos do not become repeatable workflows on their own.
Why trust breaks If the team cannot inspect runs, agent behavior feels unpredictable and risky.
Why this matters The blocker is operationalizing agents inside a product, not simply accessing a model.
The bottleneck is operationalizing agents, not accessing models 02
Proposed Solution

axiom gives teams one place to discover, create, run, and inspect agents.

Instead of stitching together prompts, SDK code, and internal tools, teams get a single local-first product with an agent library, a custom builder, and built-in observability.
